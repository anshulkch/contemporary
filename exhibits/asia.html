Asia


Militaristic Culture

Japanese Militaristic cultural shift throughout world war II. The rise of General Tojo led to a complete ideological shift as Japan grew more and more nationalistic, leading to their invasion of a large part of China and Manchuria. This also lead to the attack on pearl harbor, which led to US involvement.




Hiroshima & Nagasaki


Bombing of Hiroshima, 1945. The decision made by US President Harry Truman to drop the Atomic Bomb upon Japan’s Hiroshima is considered the final act of World War II. After this and the bombing of Nagasaki, Japan surrendured, officially ending the war. This exhibit is a short film outlining the construction and testing of the hydrogen bomb and its plans to be used to end World War II.




Mao Zedong


This exhibit consists of propaganda used in China in 1949 during the Communist revolution. This revolution saw Mao Zedong’s rise to power, and propaganda like this was used to influence civilians to support his rise to power.




Asian Conflict


Vietnam and Korean Wars. China wasn’t the only country to try out Communism. Korea had intense fighting for many years, splitting the free market South Korea from the totalitarian North Korea. Vietnam has a similar civil war. The United States intervenes in both of these countries, and after many years of fighting, Korea remained split, where it still is today. However, Vietnam reunited under communism and is still there today.
